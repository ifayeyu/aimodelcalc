## Verdict at a glance

The site is **structurally in good shape for AdSense** — it has real interactive tools, ~14,000 words of original guide content, and every policy page Google looks for (Privacy, Terms, Contact, About, Editorial Policy). It is well clear of the "low value content" trap that flagged the sister site.

**One issue dominates everything else: the pricing data is critically out of date**, and the site loudly claims to be "live." It lists models that were *shut down on June 1, 2026* (Gemini 1.5/2.0) and omits the entire current generation (GPT‑5.x, Claude 4.x, Gemini 3.x). For a site whose entire value proposition is pricing accuracy, this is both an AdSense quality risk and a user-trust problem. Fix this first.

Severity key: 🔴 Critical (fix before review) · 🟠 High · 🟡 Medium · 🟢 Low/polish

## 1. Pricing accuracy — 🔴 Critical

This is the heart of the site and the biggest liability. The homepage badge says **"Updated April 2026 — Live pricing"** (today is June 20, 2026), and the calculator's model list is roughly a year behind reality.

### What's wrong

- **Lists discontinued models.** The calculator includes **Gemini 1.5 Flash, Gemini 1.5 Pro, and Gemini 2.0 Flash**. Per Google's own pricing page, **Gemini 2.0 Flash and 2.0 Flash‑Lite were shut down June 1, 2026**, and the 1.5 family is older still. A "live" calculator offering models that no longer exist is the worst-case version of stale.
- **Omits the entire current generation.** No GPT‑5.x, no Claude 4.x (Opus/Sonnet/Haiku), no Gemini 3.x. These are the models people are actually pricing in June 2026.
- **Internal contradiction.** The homepage info cards and the `gpt-4o-pricing` guide already reference **"Gemini 2.5 Flash," "Gemini 2.5 Pro," and "Claude Sonnet 4"** — models that **don't exist in the calculator's own data table**. The guides and the tool disagree with each other.
- **Stale flagship pricing.** Even where models still exist, the headline lineup (GPT‑4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Claude 3 Opus) is now legacy/retired pricing, not the current frontier.

## 2. AdSense policy compliance — 🟠 High

Good news first: the foundations Google checks are present and genuinely solid.

**Already compliant ✅**
- **Required pages all present and substantive:** Privacy Policy (with cookies/advertising/analytics disclosure), Terms, Contact (real email + response times), About (named author, real bio), plus an Editorial Policy and Pricing Methodology — more than most applicants have.
- **Original, useful content:** three working calculators + ten 1,200–1,700-word guides. This is real value, not thin/scraped content.
- **`ads.txt` correct and Authorized:** `google.com, pub-6095140459786991, DIRECT, f08c47fec0942fa0`.
- **Affiliate disclosure done right:** the NordVPN block is clearly labeled "Sponsored" and uses `rel="noopener sponsored"`.
- **AdSense loader present on every page.**

**Issues to fix**

- 🟠 **Placeholder "Advertisement" boxes with no real ad.** Every page renders dashed grey boxes reading "Advertisement" / "Advertisement · 300 × 250". There are **zero real `<ins class="adsbygoogle">` units** anywhere. Two problems: (a) labeling an empty box "Advertisement" can read as deceptive/manufactured ad inventory to a reviewer, and (b) it wastes layout on dead space. Recommendation: **remove the fake placeholder boxes for the review.** After approval, replace them with genuine AdSense units (Auto ads or manual `<ins>`), keeping a reasonable content-to-ad ratio.
- 🟠 **Accuracy = a policy issue, not just UX.** AdSense's policies cover misrepresentative/low-value content. A prominent "Live pricing" claim attached to discontinued models is exactly the kind of inaccuracy that undermines a "Getting ready" review. §1 is therefore also an AdSense item.
- 🟡 **Sister-site context.** Your AdSense dashboard shows `darlingwhatif.com` flagged "Low value content." Approvals are per-site, but it's worth making sure aimodelcalc doesn't share thin templates or boilerplate with it.
- 🟢 No popups, no adult/prohibited content, no copyrighted material spotted.

## 4. SEO — 🟡 Medium

Strong technical baseline; a few fixes.

**Already good ✅** — canonical tags, Open Graph + Twitter cards, JSON‑LD (`WebApplication` + `WebSite` with named author), `sitemap.xml`, `robots.txt` with sitemap reference, descriptive titles/meta descriptions, clean apex-domain URLs (no stray `www`), semantic headings, Google Analytics installed.

**Fix**
- 🟡 **Freshness signals contradict each other and the calendar.** Homepage badge says "Updated April 2026," guides say "May 2026," it's now June. Search engines (and users) read this as neglected. Standardize on the true last-updated date sitewide and bump it whenever data changes.
- 🟡 **`sitemap.xml` `lastmod` dates are frozen at 2026‑05‑06/07** and the sitemap is missing some URLs — e.g. `about.html` is listed but the root-level `gpt-4o-pricing.html` / `cheapest-ai-api.html` / `pricing-methodology.html` redirect stubs and a couple of guides should be reconciled. Regenerate after content updates.
- 🟡 **Add `FAQPage` and `Article` structured data.** The FAQ page is ideal for `FAQPage` schema; each guide should carry `Article`/`TechArticle` with `datePublished`/`dateModified`. This is high-ROI for a pricing/reference site and can win rich results.
- 🟢 **`<meta name="keywords">`** on the homepage is ignored by Google and signals dated SEO — harmless, but worth removing.
- 🟢 **Add descriptive `lastmod` + consider breadcrum"** structured data on guides for sitelink eligibility.

## 5. UX & accessibility — 🟡 Medium

The design is clean, fast (no framework, self-contained), dark-theme consistent, and the tools are genuinely useful. Mobile breakpoints exist throughout.

**Fix**
- 🟠 **`h1 { white-space: nowrap }` on the homepage hero** ("Compare AI Model Costs Instantly") risks horizontal overflow / forced shrinking on narrow phones. Remove `nowrap` and let it wrap.
- 🟡 **Color contrast.** Muted body text `#8b949e` on dark `#0d1117`/`#1c2333` is borderline against WCAG AA for small text. Nudge the muted tone lighter (e.g. toward `#a9b2bd`) for the long editorial paragraphs.
- 🟡 **Calculators have no labels/ARIA for screen readers beyond visual `<label>`s**, and results update silently. Consider `aria-live="polite"` on the results panel so changes are announced.
- 🟡 **Empty/placeholder ad boxes** (see §2) also hurt UX by pushing content down with dead space.
- 🟢 **Dead-end redirect stubs.** Root-level `gpt-4o-pricing.html`, `cheapest-ai-api.html`, `pricing-methodology.html` are meta-refresh redirects to `/guides/…`. Fine, but a server-side 301 is cleaner for SEO than a `<meta http-equiv="refresh">`. Low priority on GitHub Pages.
- 🟢 **404 page** is well done — on-brand, with helpful links back.
